## Dataset lifecycle

- **$\mathcal{D}_{\text{pre}}$** — tens of thousands of hours across many robots/tasks; frozen after pre-training (provides common sense).
- **$\mathcal{D}_{\text{demo}}$** — ~50–100 expert teleoperated trajectories per task (the goal); collected once.
- **$\mathcal{D}_{\text{practice}}$** — the robot's own autonomous rollouts (successes *and* failures) + expert interventions mid-mistake; grows every RL iteration.
- **Advantage indicator $I_t$** — recomputed each iteration over the pool $\mathcal{D}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{D}_{\text{demo}} \cup \mathcal{D}_{\text{practice}}$: re-run the updated value function on every action, recompute the advantage $A_t$, and reassign $I_t$.

## Rewards and returns

$$
r_t = \begin{cases} 0 & t = T,\ \text{success} \\ -C_{\text{fail}} & t = T,\ \text{failure} \\ -1 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\qquad
R_t = \sum_{t'=t}^{T} r_{t'}
$$

## Distributional value function

Discretise the return into $B = 201$ bins and train a VLM-backbone classifier $p_\phi(V \mid o_t, \ell) \in \mathbb{R}^{201}$ by cross-entropy:

$$
\mathcal{L}(\phi) = \mathbb{E}_{\tau \in \mathcal{D}}\Big[ \sum_{o_t \in \tau} H\big(R_t^B,\ p_\phi(V \mid o_t, \ell)\big) \Big]
$$

(equivalent to minimising $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q \,\|\, p)$, since $H(q,p) = H(q) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\|p)$). Recover a scalar value as the expectation over bin centres $c_b$:

$$
V_\phi(o_t, \ell) = \sum_{b=1}^{B} p_\phi(b \mid o_t, \ell)\, c_b
$$

Distributional classification captures uncertainty and trains more stably than direct scalar regression.

## Advantage and policy

The $N$-step advantage:

$$
A_t = \Big( \sum_{i=t}^{t+N-1} r_i + V_\phi(o_{t+N}, \ell) \Big) - V_\phi(o_t, \ell)
$$

Per task, threshold at the 30th percentile $\epsilon_\ell$ and label $I_t = \mathbf{1}_{A_t > \epsilon_\ell}$ (better-than-average move). Train the policy with a standard imitation term plus an advantage-conditioned term:

$$
\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{D}}\big[ -\log \pi_\theta(a_t \mid o_t, \ell) - \alpha \log \pi_\theta(a_t \mid I_t, o_t, \ell) \big]
$$

A **stop-gradient** between the continuous action expert and the VLM backbone (knowledge insulation) preserves the pretrained representations. The action head is hybrid: discrete tokens (NLL) for gripper/subtask channels + a flow-matching branch for the continuous horizon $a_{t:t+H}$ (target field $\omega - a_{t:t+H}$).

## Iterate and deploy

- **Task initialisation** — fine-tune the generalist on ~50 demos ($I_t = 1$ for all) → $\pi_\ell^{0}$.
- **Iterative improvement** — deploy $\pi_\ell^{k-1}$ (feeding $I_t = 1$ so it attempts high-advantage behaviour), collect autonomous rollouts + human interventions, retrain the value function, recompute advantages, relabel ($I_t = 1$ for interventions and high-advantage actions), and refine the policy — repeat until the success/robustness target is met.
- **Inference** — set $I_t = 1$ and combine the conditional and unconditional scores via classifier-free guidance:

$$
\nabla_a \log \hat{\pi} = \nabla_a \log \pi_\theta(a \mid o, \ell) + \beta\big( \nabla_a \log \pi_\theta(a \mid I{=}1, o, \ell) - \nabla_a \log \pi_\theta(a \mid o, \ell) \big)
$$
