## Introduction
In ML, there are two methods of inferences: induction and transduction.  
Inductive learning is where the model learns a general rule $$f: X \to Y$$ from training data, and then applying it to any unseen test data. 
Transductive learning predicts labels only for the specific test points provided at training time. The goal is to predict $$Y_{\text{test}}$$ without learning a general function $$f$$.  

Traditional sequence transduction models, such as RNN, LSTM, or GRU, receive previous output as an input. 
Due to their inherently sequential computation nature, these models cannot be fully parallelrised, limiting computational efficiency. 
The authors propose a new architecture called the Transformer, which completely eliminates recurrence. 
Instead, it uses attention mechanisms only to capture dependencies across sequences.

## Background
Prior attempts to reduce sequential computation in models like ByteNet, ConvS2S, and Extended Neural GPU using convolutions still have limitations in capturing long-range dependencies efficiently. On the other hand, self-attention can directly connect all positions in a sequence with constant-time operations and parallelizability, outperforming RNN/CNN methods in both speed and scalability.
